## FOSS-first Policy

Jo introduced agenda item 17, a FOSS-first policy suggestion. He clarified that this policy aims to prioritize FOSS tooling without prohibiting proprietary tools, citing the EU's push for sovereignty and the Software Freedom Conservancy's scoring of funding applications based on tool usage. Jo believes a FOSS-first policy will help the FPA allocate funding and build on existing FOSS integrations, while also acknowledging potential funding vulnerabilities and the exclusion of users who avoid proprietary tools. 

Turan argued for a "human-first" policy, suggesting that people use tools like Discord and YouTube because they are familiar, even if they are not FOSS. Jo agreed that the loss of human interaction due to the lack of FOSS options is a factor, but emphasized that offering both FOSS and proprietary options allows for user choice. Yorik supported the FOSS-first policy as a written guideline for the FPA, distinguishing between individual tool choices and the FPA's organizational practices. He stressed that the FPA should prioritize FOSS for its own operations and investments. Chris Rogers inquired about alternative platforms for YouTube to increase accessibility. Jo suggested MakerTube, a PeerTube instance, as a FOSS video hosting option, while acknowledging YouTube's reach. He also mentioned the need for a FreeCAD email account for associated accounts. John raised concerns about the vagueness of "try to" in the policy, the importance of making alternatives accessible, and the need for a dedicated social media manager with documented account access and content guidelines. Chris Rogers volunteered to take on the social media management role. Brad highlighted the importance of considering both the feature set and the practicality of FOSS tools, noting the current reliance on GitHub for developer communication and the potential friction of moving away from it. He suggested a survey of current tools and alternatives.

## Donation Platform and Currency

Yorik discussed switching default donations to Euros instead of Dollars, noting that GitHub and OpenCollective only accept USD. He proposed a solution similar to Blender's, using Stripe as the primary donation widget on the website, allowing users to choose between USD and EUR, and then linking to other platforms like Open Collective. Brad expressed interest in this approach and also noted increased inquiries about crypto donations during the release. Yorik cautioned that maintaining crypto donations could be too much work. Turan mentioned that Wikimedia, a large FOSS organization, canceled its cryptocurrency donations. John inquired about other platforms like Patreon for recurring donations. Yorik stated that the website renewal would address the donation currency issue.
