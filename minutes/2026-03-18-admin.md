### Trademark

Yorik reported on item 11, the FPA trademark, stating he is preparing a counter-argumentation to the decision to decline the trademark for "FreeCAD." The declination cited "free" and "CAD" as common words. Yorik's arguments include: the combination of these words is trademarkable with precedents like Greenpeace, FreeCAD already has a logo trademark, and "FreeCAD" unmistakably refers only to their software when searched. Chris Rogers suggested adding the camel case capitalization (FreeCAD) as an identifiable part of the name. John, as a Devil Advocate, noted that people do search for "FreeCAD" and find the software. Yorik clarified that people search for "free beer" but not necessarily together, and that "FreeCAD" as a term is unique to their software. Chris also suggested that if the AI brings up alternatives when searching for "FreeCAD software," this could be used as an argument.
