# FreeCAD Project Association Admin Team Meeting

Wednesday, 6 May 2026 

Bigger Blue Button online meeting

_00:05:15 – 00:07:18_ OpenCascade 8 is nearing release, with performance testing underway; Pieter expects final changes within two days. Chris Hennes decides to delay rebuilding the libpack with OpenCascade 8 beta until the final release is available.

_00:10:50 – 00:14:05_ Frank Martínez introduced a new standardized add-on template for FreeCAD, built on cookie cutter, with improved structure and examples to help developers. The goal is to reduce onboarding friction by providing working templates for minimal commands, workbenches, and features.

_00:15:15 – 00:21:35_ The team discussed persistent issues with the "bigger blue button" tool, including audio permission popups, disappearing controls, and poor scalability in large meetings. While some users reported better experience than Jitsi, others noted usability problems. No immediate action was taken, but Chris Hennes committed to follow up with Benjamin Hyarian.

_00:22:18 – 00:27:47_ Joe reported a communication failure involving a candidate for the FPA community design competition role, who had applied in October and was later contacted by Joe without awareness of the prior application. This highlighted a risk in uncoordinated outreach. The team agreed on the need for a formal communication process with assigned responsibility for tracking and responding to external inquiries.

_00:30:43 – 00:31:42_ Alexandre proposed creating a governance page to guide users on where to go for different issues and who is responsible, offering to prototype the structure. The team discussed the need for a clear process to avoid missing communications, especially in recruitment and grant applications.

_00:33:04 – 00:34:31_ Brad suggested a two-phase approach: short-term process changes to prevent email loss, and long-term implementation of a CRM system using Nextcloud or third-party tools. Yorik proposed sub-emails for different functions (e.g., jobs, grants), but Brad argued this could create silos. C.Rogers supported a unified inbox with tagging or triage.

_00:43:13 – 00:45:03_ Chris introduced a proposal to recognize translators with physical tokens like t-shirts, stickers, or signed certificates. C.Rogers suggested using a lightweight CRM like LibreOffice Calc or a sticker-based system. Brad proposed a tiered system of contributor-specific stickers. Yorik cautioned about setting a precedent for other contributor groups.

_00:50:39 – 00:52:17_ C.Rogers advocated for a phased approach: start with stickers to encourage current contributions, then expand to 3D-printed keychains or custom items. Pieter suggested using a 3D model hosted on a lens server so translators could self-print items. Chris concluded by assigning the creation of a new agenda item for a dedicated merchandising meeting.

_00:55:06 – 00:56:33_ Chris Hennes inquired about the preferred donation mechanism with lowest fees, leading to discussion on PayPal’s high fees for small donations and the need to guide users toward lower-cost options. The team acknowledged the financial impact of penny donations and the need to steer donors toward better platforms.

_00:56:34 – 00:58:55_ Chris Hennes proposed hiring someone to set up a donor communication system, noting Turan’s overburdened schedule despite his willingness to help. The team agreed that paying for this role would be preferable to relying on volunteers. Jo H. expressed interest in the role, especially if it included community liaison development, and confirmed the competition was gaining traction.

_00:59:01 – 01:01:40_ Jo H. provided an update on the FreeCAD Design Competition, noting it was live and gaining visibility through CERN and LibreSpace. Feedback was given on poor navigation from the blog to the submission form, prompting a plan to improve linking. Frank Martínez raised concerns about FPA members and their families participating, leading to a decision to exclude them from the pilot.

_01:04:09 – 01:05:31_ The team discussed trademark enforcement after a fake FreeCAD site was discovered. Chris Hennes emphasized the legal obligation to act, and Yorik noted that a formal letter could be sent without legal counsel. Frank Martínez shared that OrcaSlicer faced similar issues and used banners in documentation to warn users. C.Rogers suggested using Google and Bing reporting tools to delist impersonators quickly.
