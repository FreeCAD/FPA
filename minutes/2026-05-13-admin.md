# FreeCAD Project Association Admin Team Meeting

Wednesday, 14 May 2026

Bigger Blue Button online meeting

_00:06:47 – 00:08:39_ Pieter reported on the release of OpenCascade 8, highlighting that it includes fixes for numerous issues from version 7.8, modernization of the codebase (e.g., removing "standard false/true"), and improved developer onboarding. He noted that FreeCAD is working on a PR to support both OpenCascade 7 and 8.

_00:09:06 – 00:13:09_ Frank Martínez presented the new cookie-cutter template for FreeCAD add-ons, which includes three sample structures. He raised uncertainty about whether to make it the default or keep it as an alternative due to community feedback. Chris Hennes supported using the cookie-cutter approach over GitHub-based templates to avoid tight coupling with GitHub and to simplify documentation maintenance.

_00:13:37 – 00:17:09_ A discussion emerged about establishing a formal review process for non-core repositories (e.g., add-ons, materials, machines). sliptonic (Brad) questioned whether domain experts could be involved in reviewing non-code contributions. Chris Hennes suggested that the FPA might need to fund a coordinator role. The team agreed to inventory all such repositories and assess which have formal review processes, with the topic to be discussed at the next developer meeting.

_00:20:18 – 00:25:47_ The team debated renaming the "reward eligible" tag to reduce AI spam. Chris Hennes expressed concern about discarding Kacper’s prior work in labeling issues. Alternatives like "FPA program eligible" or "FBA Hunter" were proposed. The consensus was to avoid terms like "reward" or "bounty" to reduce AI targeting. The team agreed to keep the bounty program active but rename the tag to reduce spam.

_00:26:18 – 00:26:40_ Yorik proposes restricting bounty eligibility to those who have pre-registered for the program, arguing that this would prevent spam while still allowing real contributors to access the reward list.

_00:27:40 – 00:28:19_ Chris Hennes and Reqrefusion agree that a project board accessible only to enrolled developers (e.g., "Bounty Hunters") would prevent scraping of reward-eligible PRs, and Chris suggests using a NextCloud form for enrollment.

_00:30:46 – 00:31:39_ Brad outlines plans to contact a consultant for an open-source CRM, with a potential meeting scheduled for next week (14-05-2026), to support donor management and FPA communications.

_00:34:27 – 00:35:35_ The team agrees to prioritize Stripe as the default donation method due to low fees and direct deposit, and to model the donation interface after Blender’s, with a prominent Stripe button and optional "other methods" link.

_00:44:21 – 00:44:56_ Domain recovery: Billy Huddleston explained that Jurgen, as the current domain owner, must provide proof of ownership (e.g., notarized letter or ID) to the new registrar to initiate transfer. Yorik agreed to contact Jurgen to request this cooperation.

_00:47:08 – 00:47:57_ Upcoming events: Chris Hennes confirmed the next real-world meetup is in North America, with registration open. He also mentioned planned gatherings in Peru in November and FOSDEM 2027, which remains pending due to unknown dates.

_00:48:00 – 00:48:25_ Forum migration: Chris Hennes took responsibility for launching a test Discourse forum, including backing up the current forum, ingesting data (12-hour process), and setting up moderators. He emphasized urgency before Kurt relocates.

_00:51:14 – 00:51:49_ Financial status: Yorik reported the project’s bank balance had fallen below €100,000, down from €180,000 last year, demonstrating improved spending, but also prompting a discussion about future spending and potential rebalancing.
