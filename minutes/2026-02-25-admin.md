# FreeCAD Project Association Admin Team Meeting

25 February 2026

This meeting of the FreeCAD Project Association administrative team covers infrastructure updates, community initiatives, financial discussions, and event planning. Key topics include resolving wiki styling issues, domain registrar changes, the add-on ecosystem, trademark protection, bug bounty programs, and strategies for donations and community engagement.

---

## Infrastructure and Domain Updates

Kurt, the Infrastructure Maintenance Lead, reported resolving a persistent issue with the wiki styling, describing it as a "classic case of three bugs in a trench coat combined". The problem involved request headers handled through Anubis and MediaWiki.

Additionally, Kurt noted that our domain registrar, Gandi, has increased prices. The team is considering moving to Infomaniac. The `FreeCAD.io` domain is particularly being reviewed for this transition. He also mentioned that Gandi's email services are only "so-so," suggesting a potential future move to a dedicated mail provider.

## Trademark and Branding

The team discussed the importance of trademarking "FreeCAD" to prevent brand confusion and potential misuse, especially with the possibility of forks. Benjamin provided research suggesting that while the logo with text is registered, the name "FreeCAD" itself is not. Registering the name is relatively inexpensive ($250), and extending protection to European countries and the U.S. is estimated to cost €2,000 to €4,000.

There was a concern that not registering broadly could allow others to attempt to claim the trademark, potentially resulting in further confusion and even litigation. Concern was expressed that beginning this process could result in an "arms war" where various actors attempt to register the FreeCAD name in various jurisdictions. Yorik pointed out that within the EU, even though the trademark would technically be Benelux-zone only, that would still make it more difficult for someone else to assert it as a trademark elsewhere. The discussion also touched on the "five-year rule," where inaction against trademark infringement can lead to the loss of rights. Registering the trademark would compell the FPA to assert the trademark in the event that another organization attempted to use it. Despite this, the consensus was to proceed with registering the text trademark due to its low cost, while further discussion is needed for broader international protection.

## Financial and Donation Strategies

The financial health of the association was discussed, with concerns raised about the current exchange rate impacting dollar-denominated donations. Yorik proposed several changes to the donation system:
*   Denominating donations in Euros on the website.
*   Removing the fixed "bronze, silver, gold" donation tiers.
*   Implementing an open input field for donations.

Chris Hodges suggested that the lack of explicit donor appreciation might be impacting fundraising. Ideas for improvement include:
*   Adding a "thank you" message or prompt during the donation process.
*   Creating a dedicated blog post or a section in the release video acknowledging donors and detailing how funds are used.
*   Developing an infographic or animation video to visually represent fund allocation.

A significant concern was raised about the technical and administrative complexities of managing donor data, including privacy, anonymity requests, and data security, if a more formalized donation tracking system is implemented.

---

## Event Planning and Future Initiatives

**FOSDEM 2027:** The team discussed strategies for the FOSDEM event, acknowledging that the current venue might be outgrown and that varying the location could revitalize interest. They also debated whether to continue the tradition of holding the event the day before FOSDEM or explore alternative timing and locations, potentially in North America, South America, or even Asia. A more structured approach to speaker submissions and advertising was suggested to increase engagement and attendance.

**Bug Bounty Program:** The renewal of the bug bounty program for €5,000 was approved, with a request from Roy for €550 already pending. The team acknowledged the risk of abuse of such programs, referencing the CURL team's experience with fabricated issues, but noted that FreeCAD's program has not faced similar problems. They agreed to monitor the situation closely.

**Code Signing and Travel Grants:** Chris Hennes confirmed that the Azure-based code signing is set up and paid for via his credit card, with plans to transfer it to the association's card and onboard other team members. Yorik reported that the travel grant webpages have been updated to denominate grants in Euros.

The meeting concluded with discussions on several other agenda items, including the status of a domain name reservation, a bug bounty for test creation, ongoing issues with a university project, and the potential for more distributed FreeCAD events. The team agreed to defer more in-depth discussions on certain topics to future meetings, particularly those requiring significant planning or absent key personnel.
