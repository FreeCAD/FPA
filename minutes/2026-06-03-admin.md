# FPA Admin Team Meeting

Wednesday, 3 June 2026

## Summary

The FPA admin team convened on 3 June 2026 to address critical operational and financial challenges, beginning with the strategic decision to adopt CiviCRM as the organization’s donor and relationship management system. Following a review of its capabilities and technical feasibility, the team unanimously agreed to proceed with a proof of concept using a hosted CiviCRM solution. Alain Benbassat was assigned to draft a letter of engagement and initiate the setup process. In response to recurring scheduling conflicts, the team also agreed to shift future meetings to Thursdays starting the following week. Several calendar and link discrepancies were identified during the session and assigned to specific team members for immediate correction to ensure smoother coordination in the coming weeks.

A significant portion of the meeting was devoted to addressing long-standing concerns around financial transparency and reporting. The team highlighted persistent issues with inconsistent data presentation, lack of accessible income and expense tracking, and the absence of a centralized financial system, all of which undermine donor trust and organizational accountability. A heated but constructive debate emerged over whether the FPA is genuinely at risk of bankruptcy, with differing views on the severity of the financial situation and the urgency of corrective action. Participants emphasized the need for clearer financial policies and more accurate reporting, with a strong preference for spreadsheets over visual charts to ensure data integrity. The discussion also touched on the complexities of VAT treatment, particularly regarding grants and invoicing practices.

The conversation deepened on the financial inequity arising from how VAT is applied when grants are issued with or without invoices. There was growing concern that recipients who issue invoices effectively receive more funds due to the VAT component, creating an uneven playing field. The team acknowledged the need for further data analysis before making policy decisions and agreed to revisit the issue in a follow-up session. The role of the upcoming CiviCRM implementation in resolving donor and financial tracking issues was underscored as central to long-term improvement. In parallel, the team addressed risks related to domain and digital asset ownership, particularly concerning freecadweb.org. There were concerns about the domain’s long-term security and the potential for loss due to lack of clear ownership documentation. The team discussed the possibility of reclaiming the domain through legal means, should necessary documentation be produced.

The meeting concluded with a brief but decisive exchange on digital infrastructure and community assets. Chris Hennes committed to contacting Kurt to initiate a database cleanup on the FreeCAD forums, independent of the broader financial and administrative issues, to improve site hygiene and user experience. C Rogers shared a new video and accompanying description that had been added to the conference assets, highlighting recent activities at the Open Hardware Summit and reinforcing the importance of maintaining up-to-date public records. The session ended on a collaborative note, with the team reaffirming its commitment to improving transparency, strengthening systems through CiviCRM, and securing digital assets to support the FPA’s long-term sustainability and credibility.

NOTE: Future meetings will be held on Thursdays at the same time to accomodate schedule changes from some admin team members.
