## Vectorspace FRC Robotics Team Report

Adam Spontarelli, head coach of the FRC robotics team in Virginia, presented on their season and their experience using FreeCAD. He was joined by two students, Bennett and Nikos, who shared their perspectives.

*   **Team Overview:** The FRC team competes in a sport-like robotics competition for high schoolers. The FPA generously sponsored the team, and the presentation covered the completed season, which ran from January to the previous weekend.
*   **Robot Design and FreeCAD Implementation:** The team's final robot was presented both in real-life photos and within FreeCAD. The robot comprised several sub-assemblies:
    *   **Drive Base:** Designed primarily by Bennett, this included the frame, electronics housing, and the innovative bumper assembly. This bumper design won an award at their first competition. Bennett noted challenges with importing STEP files from purchased Swerve modules, which often caused lag.
    *   **Shooter:** Nikos worked on the shooter, which included complex sketches for weight savings. He shared that while the CAM workbench was robust, the sketcher became laggy with numerous constraints, sometimes requiring significant reload times.
    *   **Indexer and Intake:** These mechanisms were responsible for collecting and feeding the game balls into the shooter.
*   **FreeCAD Experience and Challenges:**
    *   **General Usage:** The students found FreeCAD to be a good learning tool, though they encountered minor bugs. The CAM workbench was praised as robust and intuitive, with Bennett noting it exceeded the capabilities of Onshape's free tier.
    *   **Assembly Workbench Issues:** The assembly workbench presented the most significant challenges. The team struggled with creating flexible sub-assemblies and animating the robot's movements, particularly when integrating components from external STEP files.
    *   **Measurement Tool:** Bennett highlighted that Onshape's "quick measure" tool provided more comprehensive and intuitive measurements compared to FreeCAD's, especially when measuring between points and projected geometry.
    *   **Offset Geometry:** A specific issue involved the offset tool in Sketcher not working correctly on projected geometry from shape binders, which was identified as a kernel limitation.
    *   **Weight Savings and CAM:** Nikos discussed the process of creating weight savings through numerous triangles, a "user error" that led to a sloppy plate when trying to cut them out. They also discussed the challenge of preventing parts from kicking up during CAM cutting and the decision to use a high safe height for the router.
*   **Outcome and Future Use:** The team successfully completed their season, winning awards for their bumper design and the robot itself. They plan to continue using FreeCAD due to its strengths and the responsiveness of the FreeCAD community.

## First Quarter Grant Results

From Turan: The grant results for the first quarter are out, and we've once again accepted Paddle's dual development grant. We've been accepting this grant for a year now. Specifically, we introduced a single-proposal rule for this grant this year. But Paddle circumvented it through brute force, and we ignored it.
* The quarterly budget wasn't sufficient, so we found a way around that by canceling another grant, thus supposedly adhering to our own rules.
* However, I believe we shouldn't continue like this. We need to make a definitive decision and stop constantly postponing it to the next quarter.
* Furthermore, two valuable members of the community (Kadet and FEAEng) have clearly expressed their full support for these grants and the logic behind them, and Paddle, being a valuable member, is the one making these proposals. Given the community's support and our repeated acceptance of this grant for a year, we must make the logical decision.

Others on the team expressed skepticism about funding a permanent development position, rather than focusing funding on the "boring" support roles. FreeCAD has been developed by volunteeer for its whole existence, and the FPAs money may best be spent on the work that volunteers don't typically enjoy (bug triage, documentation, infrastructure maintenance, etc.) Many developers would be interested in getting additional income for their work on FreeCAD, and questions about how we'd choose PaddleStroke over anyone else for this position arose. No action item was generated by this discussion, which was tabled for future consideration.
